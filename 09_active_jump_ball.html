2.9 JUMP BALL - ACTIVE REFEREE

FIBA Referees Manual - Chapter 2: IOT

Jump-ball situations are exciting, but unfortunately sometimes the administrating referee is unable to toss the ball properly. Analysis indicates that 50% of the violations during the jump-ball are caused by a poor toss by the referee. In other words, they could have been avoided. Tossing the ball is something that needs to be trained on a regular basis, unlike what is currently the case. It is crucial to understand that the jumpers are tense and will react easily on any movement caused by the referee. So therefore it is crucial to avoid any unnecessary movements.

A checklist for a successful ball toss:

  • Players are tense - avoid any extra movements

The failure to adhere to these principles is the primary reason for many incorrect decisions on the floor. Consistently maintaining proper coverage improves the overall quality of officiating.
