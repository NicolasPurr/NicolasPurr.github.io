2.1 INTRODUCTION

FIBA Referees Manual - Detailed Chapter

Individual Officiating Techniques (IOT) is the most important foundation for either 2 Person Officiating (2PO) or 3 Person Officiating (3PO). Over the past 15 years, there has been a heavy focus on 3PO (namely for the mechanical movements of the referees on the floor), which has led to a lack of knowledge in how to referee individual play phases.

These inherent skills of IOT are equally relevant for both 2PO and 3PO. In analysing play phases and calling the game, some basic principles are necessary:

  • Distance & Stationary: Have proper distance from the play(See also our Distance & Stationary chapter.)
  • Referee the Defence: Focus on illegal actions by the defensive player. (Refer to Refereeing the Defence for more details.)
  • Active Mind-set: Maintain a view of as many players as possible in the play.
  • 45Â° & Open Angle: Know when the play has ended so you can transition to the next phase.
  • Stay with the Play until it is over: Do not release focus before the play concludes.

The failure to adhere to these principles is the primary reason for many incorrect decisions on the floor. Consistently maintaining proper coverage improves the overall quality of officiating.
