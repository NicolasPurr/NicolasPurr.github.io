Individual Officiating Techniques

2.1 INTRODUCTION

Individual Officiating Techniques (IOT) is the most important foundation for either 2 Person Officiating (2PO) or 3 Person Officiating (3PO). It is evident that in the past 15 years there has been a heavy focus on 3PO (namely for the mechanical movements of the referees on the floor). This has led to a lack of knowledge in how to actually referee individual play phases in the game â€“ this being the fundamental skills that referees need to possess in order to process and facilitate the correct decision.

Some basic principles are necessary to follow: Have proper distance from the play, keeping an open angle and remaining stationary. Do not move too close to the play and narrow your field of vision.

2.2 DISTANCE & STATIONARY

Target: To identify and understand the key points and impact of maintaining a proper distance from the play and being stationary when refereeing competitive match ups.

  • Maintain an appropriate distance (3-6 meters) to lower reaction calls.
  • Stay stationary to keep a wide angle and full concentration.
  • This approach increases your ability to see the big picture and all players in the action.

2.3 REFEREEING THE DEFENCE

Target: To identify the primary focus when analysing a 1 on 1 play. Always focus on the illegality of the defensive player while keeping the offensive ball handler in your field of vision.

2.6 PRE-GAME CONFERENCE AND USE OF FIBA IREF PG APP

A pre-game conference is absolutely necessary to ensure you and your partner(s) are on the same page. Some discussion topics include areas of responsibility, positioning, and the usage of the FIBA iRef Pre-Game App.

Referees must communicate decisions clearly with standard hand signals and verbal support. Practice the correct whistle technique and follow through with appropriate signals.
